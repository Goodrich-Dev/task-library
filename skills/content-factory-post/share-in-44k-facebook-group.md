# Share in 44K Facebook group

**Use this when** the article is live and the client has been notified (Step 15 done) — community amplification is Step 16 of the Blog Posting process.

## Inputs
- Published article URL and the client's name/handle
- Posting access to the 44,000-member Facebook group
- The customer question the article answers (its hook)

## Steps
1. Write the group post as a **lesson, not a promo**: lead with the question the article answers or the surprising result, in plain first-person language. Group members are practitioners — give them the takeaway up front.
2. Include one concrete detail or number from the piece so the post has standalone value, then link the article for the full process.
3. Tag the client and featured people if they are group members, so they can respond and share.
4. Attach a real image or the native video clip — no stock, no banner graphics.
5. Publish, then reply to **every** comment within 24 hours; questions raised here are future one-minute video topics (feed them back to the Topic Wheel).
6. Log engagement (reactions, comments, shares) after 48 hours — strong group response marks the piece as a boost candidate for Promote's top 3–5 shortlist.

## Definition of done (QA checklist)
- [ ] Post live in the group, framed as a lesson with standalone value, article linked
- [ ] Client/featured members tagged; real image or native video attached
- [ ] Every comment answered within 24 hours; new questions captured as future topics
- [ ] 48-hour engagement logged and fed to the Promote boost shortlist
- [ ] Linked back to the definitive article and relevant siblings
- [ ] Complies with Blog Posting Guidelines (if it publishes content)

## Run on a persistent agent (Fable 5)
A persistent agent (Fable 5 or comparable OpenAI/Google models) frames the post as a lesson using the hook from memory, then keeps the run open across the 24- and 48-hour marks — answering every comment, harvesting new questions into the Topic Wheel, and logging engagement — because the Definition of done includes those follow-ups, and the run isn't done until each box passes.
Captured questions and engagement numbers persist in memory, compounding into better topics and a sharper boost shortlist with every share.
A meta-article example is logged per run so the library compounds.
