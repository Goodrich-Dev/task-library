# Create a 3-minute WHY video

**Use this when** a brand entering SAE Stage 3 (Content) has its mission defined from Stage 2 (Goals) but no single video that explains WHY it exists.

## Inputs
- The mission statement and 90-day goals from SAE Stage 2 (Goals come before Content — GCT in order)
- The Topic Wheel WHY ring (core purpose) if already mapped
- A phone, a real setting tied to the story (the shop floor, the first truck, the home office)
- The founder/owner on camera — the WHY belongs to a person, not a logo

## Steps
1. Pull the mission from Stage 2 Goals. If you cannot state the WHY in one sentence, stop and finish Goals first — Content never precedes Goals.
2. Outline three beats, no script: origin story (the moment that started this) → who you serve and what you believe → what that means for the customer.
3. Shoot on the phone in a real location connected to the story. Unscripted but rehearsed out loud once or twice — conviction, not polish.
4. Keep it to roughly 3 minutes. Long enough for a real story, short enough to hold attention and run as an ad later.
5. Watch it back once and ask one question: would a stranger understand why this person does this work? If yes, ship it; do not chase a perfect take.
6. Name the file `YYYY-MM-DD-brandname-why-video` and upload to the Content Library Drive folder.
7. Log it in the Content Library tracker flagged "WHY video — SAE Stage 3 cornerstone." It anchors the 3×3 grid and later becomes warm-audience content in Dollar a Day sequencing.
8. Queue it for the Process stage — the WHY video becomes the About-page embed, a blog post, and ad creative.

## Definition of done (QA checklist)
- [ ] WHY is stated plainly within the first 30 seconds
- [ ] Contains a specific origin story, not generic mission-speak
- [ ] Shot on phone, first person, founder on camera, real location
- [ ] Runtime ~3 minutes
- [ ] Uploaded to the Content Library, flagged as the Stage 3 cornerstone, queued for Process
- [ ] Linked back to the definitive article and relevant siblings

## Run on a persistent agent (Fable 5)

A persistent agent (Claude Fable 5, or a comparable OpenAI/Google model that loops and holds memory) enforces the order: it pulls the Stage 2 mission from memory, refuses to proceed if Goals aren't set, preps the three beats, then self-verifies the take against the Definition of done — WHY stated inside 30 seconds, real origin story, ~3 minutes — and loops until every box passes. Memory keeps the mission and prior takes, so revisions build on what worked instead of restarting. Close the run with a logged meta-article example.
