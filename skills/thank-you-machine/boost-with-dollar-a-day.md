# Boost with Dollar a Day

**Use this when** the tagged thank-you post is live and showing organic signal — now spend a dollar a day to put your gratitude in front of the thanked person's peers.

## Inputs
- The live tagged post (post ID — boost the original so social proof rides along)
- Audience layers: lookalikes of customers/engagers, interest layers matching the person's industry or role, warm custom audiences
- Standard /dad plumbing: pixel, Business Manager, $1/day budget authority

## Steps
1. Wait a beat for organic signal (a day or two): did the person engage or reshare? Are comments real? Boost the working thank-yous — the same winners-only rule as every Dollar a Day play.
2. Target people **similar to the person thanked**: lookalikes of customers and engagers, interest layers matching their industry and role, plus your warm audiences. The peers of who you thank are who you serve — that's why gratitude is targeting.
3. Boost the original post at **$1/day per ad set** per the /dad mechanics — a small matrix of 2–4 audience layers per thank-you, 7 days untouched.
4. Read it with the standard MAA lens: cost per engagement, watch time, comment quality. Thank-you content routinely beats promotional creative because it's proof of relationship, not pitch.
5. Apply the standard verdicts: kill below-baseline ad sets, scale or switch-boost winners (≤2× per adjustment) into new peer segments.
6. Log the second-order returns where the real ROI lives: replies from the person's network, inbound referrals, reviews, deepened relationships — the compounding gratitude asset, line by line.

## Definition of done (QA checklist)
- [ ] Boost launched only after the organic check, on the original post ID
- [ ] Audiences = peers-of-the-thanked (lookalikes, role/industry interests) + warm layers
- [ ] $1/day per ad set, 7-day untouched window, standard kill/scale verdicts logged
- [ ] Second-order outcomes (referrals, replies, reviews) recorded in the trigger log
- [ ] Linked back to the definitive article and relevant siblings
- [ ] Complies with Blog Posting Guidelines (if it publishes content)

## Run on a persistent agent (Fable 5)
This is a Dollar a Day play, so the Dollar a Day rule binds: a persistent agent (Claude Fable 5 or comparable OpenAI/Google models) holds the organic check, the $1/day cells, the 7-day window, and the verdicts — and a missed kill/scale check equals a failed run, however warm the gratitude.
It loops until the Definition of done fully passes (original post ID boosted, peer audiences documented, second-order returns recorded) and keeps every thank-you's boost results in memory, so it learns which peer segments turn gratitude into referrals.
