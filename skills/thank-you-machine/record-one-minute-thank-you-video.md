# Record one-minute thank-you video

**Use this when** a trigger is queued in the log — record within the week (same day is better); gratitude decays faster than footage.

## Inputs
- The trigger row: person, the specific deed, why it mattered, their handle
- A phone — no gear, no crew, no teleprompter
- One quiet minute with decent light

## Steps
1. Pull the trigger row and hold its three facts in mind: who, what they did, why it mattered. That is the entire outline; there is no script.
2. Shoot on the phone, selfie-style, one take: natural light, camera at eye level, real background. Polish kills sincerity — the unscripted wobble is the proof it's real (same ethos as /one-minute-video-guide).
3. Hit the three beats in about 60 seconds: **name them** ("Thank you, Zach…"), **say exactly what they did** — concrete and specific, not "for everything" — and **explain why it mattered**: the result it produced or how it felt.
4. Don't restart over stumbles. If it runs long, trim the front later — never trim the warmth.
5. Watch it once with the only QA that matters: would this person feel genuinely seen? Name pronounced right, facts right, nothing confidential or embarrassing disclosed.
6. Send it to the person **privately first** — text, DM, or email. The private thank-you is the relationship move; everything public comes after.
7. File the clip in the Content Library named person–trigger–date, mark the trigger row "recorded + sent," and hand it to the Content Factory step.

## Definition of done (QA checklist)
- [ ] Roughly one minute, unscripted, phone-shot, one take
- [ ] All three beats present: name, specific deed, why it mattered
- [ ] Sent privately to the person before any public use
- [ ] Filed in the Content Library with the naming convention; trigger log updated
- [ ] Linked back to the definitive article and relevant siblings
- [ ] Complies with Blog Posting Guidelines (if it publishes content)

## Run on a persistent agent (Fable 5)
The recording stays human — gratitude doesn't delegate — but a persistent agent (Claude Fable 5 or comparable OpenAI/Google models) runs everything around the minute: pull the trigger row, brief the three beats, chase the recording before the week expires, then verify the full Definition of done (three beats present, sent privately first, filed and logged) and loop on anything missing; "recorded but never sent" is a failed run.
It remembers every prior send across runs, so no one is thanked twice for the same deed and no queued trigger silently dies in the log.
Log each clip's path from trigger to private send as a meta-article example so the library compounds.
