# Create and send Thank You Machine videos

**Use this when** someone did something worth acknowledging during the campaign — a referral, a review, a share, advice, a milestone — and that gratitude can become amplification content.

## Inputs
- A trigger moment: milestone, referral, review, advice received, or a problem someone solved
- A phone (no production gear) and the recipient's name and social handle
- Access to the brand's social channels and Dollar a Day setup

## Steps
1. Catch the trigger fast — gratitude has a half-life. Same day beats next week.
2. Record a **one-minute phone video, unscripted**: name the person, say exactly what they did, explain why it mattered to you or the result it produced. Specific beats polished.
3. Send it directly first (DM, email, or text) — the private thank-you is the relationship move.
4. Post it publicly and **tag the person** so they see it and share it with their own audience — their share is the amplification.
5. Process it through the Content Factory: social post, blog excerpt, testimonial pull, ad creative, and email mention as fits.
6. Boost the public post with **$1/day** to reach people similar to the person thanked (their peers are your audience).
7. Log the trigger → video → response chain; recurring trigger types belong in the post-project checklist and weekly routine.

## Definition of done (QA checklist)
- [ ] Video is one minute, unscripted, names the person + what they did + why it mattered
- [ ] Sent privately AND posted publicly with the person tagged
- [ ] Repurposed through the Content Factory; $1/day boost running behind the public post
- [ ] Trigger and outcome logged (shares, replies, reciprocal mentions)
- [ ] Linked back to the definitive article and relevant siblings
- [ ] Complies with Blog Posting Guidelines (if it publishes content)

## Run on a persistent agent (Fable 5)
A persistent agent (Fable 5 or comparable OpenAI/Google models) watches for triggers continuously — comments, referrals, reviews logged by sibling skills — and routes each to the owner same-day with the who/what/why brief, then runs everything after the recording: private send, public tagged post, Content Factory repurpose, $1/day boost, verifying every Definition-of-done box rather than stopping at "video sent."
Its trigger → video → response log persists in memory, so recurring trigger types become standing routine instead of lucky catches.
Each run logs a meta-article example so the library compounds.
